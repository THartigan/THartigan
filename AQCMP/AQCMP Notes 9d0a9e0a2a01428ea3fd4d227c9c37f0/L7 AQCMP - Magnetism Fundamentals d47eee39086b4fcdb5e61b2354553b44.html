The Origin of Magnetic Exchange Interactions

  • Atomic exchange: For two electrons in different orbitals which are nearly orthogonal, exchange energy JJJ﻿ is positive, so the triplet state is the ground state.
    • Hence, a ferromagnetic alignment requires J>0J>0J>0﻿. here, the triplet spatial state keeps the two electrons separate, minimising Coulomb repulsion.
  • Molecular exchange: When two electrons occupy two different orbitals which overlap, JJJ﻿ can be negative, giving. a lower energy singlet state than triplet state.
    • Antiferromagnetic alignment therefore requires J<0J<0J<0﻿, which may occur by forming lower energy, delocalised molecular orbitals.
