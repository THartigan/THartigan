Stationary Markov Processes

  • Recall the definition of a stationary process from the previous lecture; such a process may be described with a propagator;
    • A Markov process is one in which each step is independent.
  • In physics, the Wiener and Poisson processes are the most common Markov processes.
    • The Wiener process describes situations where stepping up and down have equal probability and a step must be taken.
      • This gives a Gaussian step distribution.
    • The Poisson process describes a system where steps may only be taken forwards, but steps can be missed.
      • This describes events occurring randomly in space or time with a constant average rate but independent of each other.
