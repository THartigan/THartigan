Helicity in Pion Decay

  • Somewhat surprisingly, the decay of a pion into electrons is suppressed greatly relative to the decay into muons;
    • Considering this decay in the frame of the (spin-0) pion, we see that the lepton and anti-neutrino must have spins in opposite directions and they must travel in opposite directions.
      • Then noting that the anti-neutrino is almost massless, it must be in a right-helicity state, so the lepton must also be in a right helicity state.
        • Recalling that the weak interaction only produces left chiral states, and noting that an electron is much lighter than a muon, so is always in a state very similar to that of chirality, we expect the electron decay rate to be suppressed relative to that of a muon decay.
