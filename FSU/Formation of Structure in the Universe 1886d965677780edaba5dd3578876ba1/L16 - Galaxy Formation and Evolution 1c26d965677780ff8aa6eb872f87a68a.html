L16 - Galaxy Formation and Evolution

Galactic Evolution

  • As the universe expands after recombination, there is a point where the density of neutral baryonic gas becomes large enough to form the first stars and AGNs.
    • These produce UV / X-ray emissions, which ionise the neutral hydrogen, hence the name for this epoch - reionisation.
    • This is now observable in the greatly redshifted 21cm Hydrogen line.

Feedback

  • Including feedback can help solve these problems;
    • Supernova feedback can heat the gas in the disk and prevent star formation, or may eject cold gas from the disk and put it back into the halo.
      • Sufficiently energetic supernovae can expel gas beyond the cooling radius, halting star formation.
    • Reionisation of the gas by the UV background from star formation and AGN activity can also suppress star formation rate.
