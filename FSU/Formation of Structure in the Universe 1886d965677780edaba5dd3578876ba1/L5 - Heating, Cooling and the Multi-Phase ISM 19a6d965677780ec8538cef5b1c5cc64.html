Cooling By Dust

  • Dust accounts for ∼50%\sim50\%∼50%﻿ of heavy elements in the ISM and around 1%1\%1%﻿ of baryonic mass in the galaxy. However, it also accounts for ∼40%\sim 40\%∼40%﻿ of the luminosity. Dust grains (produced by end of life stars) absorb at optical wavelengths (known as extinction) and are the solid phase of the ISM.
    • The smallest particles are large molecules like polycyclic aromatic hydrocarbons (PAHs), whilst larger particles are amorphous grains of silicates and carbon.
    • Dust grains absorb short wavelength (UV and optical) photons, which excite phonons within the grain. This gives the particle a characteristic temperature, and it then emits thermally in the far- and mid-infrared.
