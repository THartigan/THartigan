Cepheids

  • Cepheids are a class of variable stars located in the upper H-R diagram.
    • Are evolved, core helium-burning stars thought to have evolved from B or O type main sequence stars. Their magnitudes vary over an order of magnitude on periods from a few days to a few weeks.
    • There is a well-calibrated (by milky-way observations) relation between luminosity cycle period and star luminosity. Therefore, measuring the period of a Cepheid allows determination of its intrinsic brightness, which then allows for distance calculation using its observed brightness.
