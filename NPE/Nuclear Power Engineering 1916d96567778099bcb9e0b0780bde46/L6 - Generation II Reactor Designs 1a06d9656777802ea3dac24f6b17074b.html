Generation II Reactor Designs

Advanced Gas-Cooled Reactors

  • All British AGRs are graphite moderated using UO2UO_2UO2​﻿ fuel pellets at around 2-3% U235U^{235}U235﻿ enrichment encased in 1 m1\text{ m}1 m﻿ long by 14.5 mm14.5\text{ mm}14.5 mm﻿ diameter stainless steel tubes. They also use B4CB_4CB4​C﻿ control rods.
    • Much more compact than Magnox reactors due to their use of enriched fuel.
    • The use of stainless steel fuel allows a higher CO2CO_2CO2​﻿ coolant temperature than in Magnox reactors, allowing thermal efficiency around 40%40 \%40%﻿.

Pressurised Water Reactors

  • PWRs have become the dominant reactor type in most of the world, taking advantage of the good moderation and heat transfer properties of water.
    • PWRs are limited mostly by the critical temperature (cannot exist as a liquid regardless of pressure applied) of water at 374∘C374 {}^\circ C374∘C﻿, limiting thermal efficiency around 35%35\%35%﻿.
    • PWRs use 3−4%3-4\%3−4%﻿ U235U^{235}U235﻿ enriched UO2UO_2UO2​﻿ fuel pellets encased in zirconium alloy tubes.
      • Enrichment is required to overcome the relatively high neutron cross-section in H2OH_2OH2​O﻿.
    • PWRs are refuelled offline every 12-24 months, with refuelling requiring depressurisation and opening of the pressure vessel - typically taking 1-2 months.
    • Power levels are controlled using moderator boric acid, H3BO3H_3BO_3H3​BO3​﻿, dissolved in the coolant.
      • Boric acid concentration can be adjusted to counteract changes to the reactivity of the fuel as the fuel cycle proceeds.
      • Emergency shutdown uses conventional Al-In-Cd fuel rods, which can also be used to perform load following (changing output to demand), as adjusting soluble boron levels is too slow.
  • The use of water as the moderator and coolant allows much smaller core sizes, decreasing costs sufficiently to compensate for the additional enrichment costs and lower thermal efficiency.

Boiling Water Reactors

  • Allowing the water in a water-moderated and cooled reactor to boil and directly drive turbines eliminates the need for heat exchangers, which are often maintenance-heavy, and allows for lower operating pressures around 737373﻿ bar with thermal efficiencies around 34%34\%34%﻿.
    • BWRs also use UO2UO_2UO2​﻿ fuel pellets with 2−3%2-3\%2−3%﻿ enriched U235U^{235}U235﻿ encased in zircaloy.
    • They are also refuelled offline every 12-24 months in a very similar procedure to PWRs.
  • The power level can be controlled either by altering coolant / moderator flow or control rods, with the former being preferable.
    • Increasing coolant flow rate reduces coolant temperature, so reduces the amount of boiling, so increases the moderation effect (water is denser than steam, so a more effective moderator), so increases the reactivity, raising reactor power, coolant temperature and the amount of boiling.
    • This then again reduces the moderating effect until the reactor reaches a new stable equilibrium at a higher power level.
  • BWR control rods are B4CB_4CB4​C﻿-loaded cross shapes, and rise up into the reactor from below, as they are most effective if inserted into a well-moderated (water) region of the core.
  • Boiling in the core produces saturated steam with dryness fraction around 10-15%. This is passed into a separator above the core, with nearly dry steam passed to turbines and water recirculated.

Heavy Water Reactors

  • The low neutron capture cross-section of D2OD_2OD2​O﻿ makes it an excellent coolant and moderator, allowing the use of natural uranium fuel. D2OD_2OD2​O﻿, however, is expensive. Canada has developed the CANDU reactor.
    • In the CANDU reactor, the D2OD_2OD2​O﻿ moderator is contained in the calandria. The calandria has built-in tubes which are then penetrated by zircaloy pressure tubes, which themselves contain both natural UO2UO_2UO2​﻿ rods clad in zircaloy and D2OD_2OD2​O﻿ as a coolant at 100100100﻿ bar.
      • The space between the calandria built-in tubes and the pressure tubes is filled with gas to thermally insulate the moderator from D2OD_2OD2​O﻿ coolant.
      • Coolant D2OD_2OD2​O﻿ reaches a maximum temperature within the pressure tubes of around 295∘C295 {}^\circ C295∘C﻿, which is relatively low, limiting thermal efficiency to around 30%30\%30%﻿.
    • The CANDU reactor can be refuelled online, and there is only a small change in reactivity replacing burnt natural uranium fuel, keeping the reactor close to criticality.
      • Reactivity / power can be controlled by poisoning (adding neutron absorbers to) the moderator with light water, or soluble solutions of boron or gadolinium.
      • Motor-driven stainless steel control rods are used for flux shaping and stainless-steel-clad cadmium control rods are used for emergency shutdown.

Liquid Metal Fast Breeder Reactors

  • In fast reactors, there is no moderator, so fission neutrons remain fast and able to breed U238U^{238}U238﻿ to fissile Pu239Pu^{239}Pu239﻿. Due to the reduced cross-section of U235U^{235}U235﻿ and Pu239Pu^{239}Pu239﻿ at high energies, enrichment around 15%15\%15%﻿ is required to sustain criticality.
    • LMFBRs use very thin fuel rods containing a mixture of UO2UO_2UO2​﻿ and PuO2PuO_2PuO2​﻿ fuel in stainless steel cladding. These rods are thin to minimise temperature drop through the fuel.
    • The core is then surrounded by blanket assemblies containing depleted UO2UO_2UO2​﻿ in which additional fuel is bred. The core is typically very small ((d=2)×(h=1) m(d=2)\times (h=1)\text{ m}(d=2)×(h=1) m﻿).
    • A non-moderating coolant with excellent heat transfer properties like liquid sodium must be used. This is also advantageous, as there is no need for pressurisation due to the high boiling point of liquid metals.
      • This sodium becomes radioactive due to the 23Na(n,γ)24Na{}^{23}Na(n,\gamma)^{24}Na23Na(n,γ)24Na﻿ reaction, so a secondary heat exchanger is required to isolate the steam cycle.
      • Despite this, steam around 565∘C565^\circ C565∘C﻿ and 160160160﻿ bar can be generated, giving high thermal efficiency.
      • This design, however, comes with complications, as sodium reacts explosively with water and air, so the area above the core is filled with an inert gas.
    • Control is achieved using either tantalum or B4CB_4CB4​C﻿-loaded stainless steel control rods.
  • Fast breeder reactors are economically challenged, as excess global capacity makes enrichment very cheap.
